The Venue

Le Stonghe

Fossombrone • Le Marche • Italy

Picture arriving up a cypress-lined drive to a 500-year-old palazzo crowning a hilltop above Fossombrone — and for three days, the whole estate is ours. Sun-warmed stone courtyards, two swimming pools, olive groves, and panoramic views that stretch all the way to the Adriatic. This is where we’ll celebrate, together.

The Setting

Fossombrone, in Italy’s best-kept secret

Set on top of a hill in Le Marche — the region known as ‘Italy’s best-kept secret’ — the estate looks out over rolling countryside between the Apennines and the Adriatic Sea. The 15th-century palazzo and its 17th-century country house were once summer residences of the Florentine Ridolfi family, lovingly restored into luxurious apartments. Nearby lie the Renaissance city of Urbino (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the Furlo Gorge and the beaches of the Adriatic coast.

Getting Here

Travel

The easiest way to reach us is to fly into Bologna (BLQ) and drive from there — a scenic two-hour trip south into the hills of Le Marche. Ancona is the closest airport (~45 min) if you can find a connection, and Rimini, Perugia, Rome and Milan are all options too. Let us know your plans and we’ll help coordinate transfers and a spot on a group shuttle.

What if it rains?

No need to worry. The estate’s Barn is a beautiful, light-filled indoor space used as a backup, and the team watches the forecast closely and will consult with us before any decisions. Luckily, it rarely rains all day in May.

What’s the weather like?

Early May in Le Marche is mild and spring-like — daytime highs around 18–22°C (mid-60s to low-70s°F) with cooler evenings. Bring a layer for after dark and perhaps something for the occasional spring shower.

Do I need a car?

Not necessarily — most events take place on the estate. But if you’d like to explore Urbino, Fossombrone or the Adriatic coast before or after, a rental car is the easiest way to get around.
